**Step 1:  Addressing Hard Constraints**

The hard constraints are:

* **Single Assignment Per Day:**  Each nurse can only have one shift per day.
* **Under-Staffing Prevention:** Minimum required nurses per shift and skill must be met.
* **Shift Type Successions:** Certain shift transitions are forbidden (e.g., Night shift cannot be followed by Early or Late).
* **Skill Requirements:** Nurses can only be assigned shifts they are qualified for.

Let's start by creating a basic roster that meets the minimum staffing requirements. We'll then refine it to address soft constraints.  I'll use a table to represent the roster, with days of the week as columns and nurses as rows.  Shift types will be indicated (E=Early, L=Late, N=Night).  Skills will be implied based on nurse capabilities and requirements.
